Hardik Monga

Organic Patterns

Exploring patterns found in nature through various media.

Year

2021

Categories

Mixed Media Nature

Organic Patterns is a mixed-media exploration of the recurring forms and structures found in the natural world. This project examines how patterns emerge across different scales and contexts in nature, from the microscopic to the cosmic, revealing underlying principles of growth and organization.

Through a combination of photography, drawing, sculpture, and digital manipulation, this series documents and interprets patterns such as branching structures, spirals, tessellations, and fractals. Each piece focuses on a specific pattern type, investigating its manifestations across different natural systems and exploring its aesthetic and mathematical properties.

The project draws inspiration from the work of scientists and artists who have studied natural patterns, including D'Arcy Thompson, Ernst Haeckel, and Andy Goldsworthy. By highlighting the beauty and complexity of these organic forms, Organic Patterns invites viewers to consider the interconnectedness of natural systems and the universal principles that shape our world.
