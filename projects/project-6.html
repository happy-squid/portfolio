Hardik Monga

Temporal Fragments

A video series examining the perception of time through visual fragments.

Year

2020

Categories

Video Experimental

Temporal Fragments is an experimental video series that explores the subjective experience of time through fragmented visual narratives. The project investigates how our perception of time is shaped by memory, anticipation, and attention, creating a non-linear experience that challenges conventional temporal frameworks.

Each video in the series focuses on a different aspect of temporal experience, from the compression and expansion of time in moments of intense emotion to the layering of past, present, and future in memory. Through techniques such as time-lapse, slow motion, jump cuts, and superimposition, the works create visual metaphors for these complex temporal phenomena.

The project draws inspiration from philosophical and scientific investigations of time, as well as from experimental filmmakers who have explored temporal manipulation, including Maya Deren, Chris Marker, and Bill Viola. By fragmenting and reconfiguring time-based media, Temporal Fragments invites viewers to reflect on their own relationship with time and the ways in which it structures our experience of the world.
